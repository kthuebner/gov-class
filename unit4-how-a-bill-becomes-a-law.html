Referred to Committee
The bill is sent to the appropriate committee — here the committee chair has the power to consider the bill or simply let it die quietly, with no vote required.

Step 5
Committee Vote
The committee votes on whether to report the bill to the full chamber — favorably, unfavorably, or not at all. A bill that is tabled here is finished.
Will they send it to the full House or Senate? ☠ Tabled = dead

Step 6
Scheduled for Debate
In the House, the Rules Committee and Speaker control whether and how the bill reaches the floor. In the Senate, the Majority Leader holds that power.

Phase 3 — Floor Action
7
Step 7
Debate & Amendments
Members debate the bill and propose amendments on the floor. In the Senate, any member can filibuster — requiring 60 votes to end debate and proceed to a vote.
Will the bill make it to a vote? ☠ Filibuster can kill it

Phase 3 — Floor Action
8
Step 8
Floor Vote
Amendments are voted on first, then there is a vote on final passage. Requires a simple majority — 218 in the House, 51 in the Senate.
Will the bill pass? ☠ Dies on the floor

Step 9
Reconciliation
The House and Senate almost always pass slightly different versions. A conference committee of senior members from both chambers must hammer out a single compromise text — which both chambers then vote on again, up or down, no further amendments.
Will both chambers approve the new version? ☠ Irreconcilable = dead

Phase 4 — Presidential Action
10
Step 10
Presidential Decision
The President can sign the bill into law, veto it, allow it to become law unsigned after 10 days, or pocket veto it if Congress adjourns before the 10 days expire.
Will the President sign the bill into law? ☠ Vetoed or Pocket Vetoed
